The Roman Era in Tunisia:
From Conquest to Legacy

Following the fall of Carthage at the end of the Third Punic War, Tunisia became part of the Roman Republic and later the Empire. Known as Africa Proconsularis, it was a vital province for Rome, contributing significantly to its economy and culture .
Tunisia’s strategic location in the heart of the Mediterranean made it a hub for trade, military operations, and cultural exchange.
Over time, the Romans rebuilt the once-destroyed Carthage, turning it into one of the empire's largest and most prosperous cities.

Key Events During the Roman Era

146 BCE :FALL OF CARTHAGE
The Romans destroy Carthage, marking the start of their rule.

44 BCE:: THE REBUILD
Julius Caesar initiates plans to rebuild Carthage.

27 BCE: THE RISE AGAIN
Carthage becomes a thriving Roman colony under Augustus.

3RD CENTRY CE: CHRISTIANITY
Christianity spreads, with figures like Saint Cyprian
and Saint Augustine playing key roles.

439 CE: THE FALL OF ROME
The Vandals conquer Carthage, ending Roman dominance.
